**Cotton's Cheat Sheet**

**Potty Time**

- Cotton is fully potty trained, a heads up that occasionally you might have accidents at home although it rarely happens.
- His schedule: He normally goes big poo and wee first thing in the morning and have a second potty roughly one hour later. Then, for the rest of the day, as long he understands where "outside" is, he will give you a heads up when he wants to go again by standing close to the exit. At night, he normally goes the last potty around 6PM, stays on the sofa with us and then he goes to his little home to sleep until the morning time.
- He never goes on a pee pad. I always pack some on his suitcase, but I doubt he will ever use it, unless if by chance. Better chances of success would be to give him regular intervals for potty outside, but from the experience from the other pet sitters, as I said before, he just needs to understand where "outside" is and should be able to give you a heads up.
- The command he understands is "**Cotton, do you want to go** **outside**?". Stress "**outside"** and I believe he will connect it to potty time. If your balcony is safe, you can just open the door, let him go outside, close the door and leave him be. I believe he will understand after a while, and if you successfully managed to do that bridge, it will save you a lot of time because you won't have to go walk him every time for potty. You won't even need to go with him to the balcony.

**Food**

- Cotton only eats his food and treats. We don't give anything else to him. He has a whole tray of wet food with about a sprinkle of dry food per day. I usually refill by breakfast. He will not eat everything at once; he might even only finish during the next night. Don't be alarmed, this is his normal behaviour, and you should only refill his bowl of food when it is completely empty. You can split the dosage between morning and night if you prefer, but it does very little difference.
- Please use treats sparingly, we give him half of a biscuit every time he deserves some.

**Water**

- I never give tap water to him because of the issue that his breed has with tear/saliva stains. I can buy a few bottles of mineral water for you if you normally don't use it. Please let me know.

**Outings/Socializing/Play Time**

- Now that the sun is out, we are taking Cotton more often to the park to socialize with other dogs and to run freely on the greens when there is not many people around. As always feel free to do whatever you think is best if you want to take him to walks, etc, not an issue, I'd just stay away from mud, if possible.
- He loves fetching his ball or playing tug with the rope. On a cold, rainy day, playing with him on a little space to fetch and tug is more than enough for him to burn his energy. He only requires 30 minutes of activity because he is so small.
- Cotton is fine on public transport and/or crowded places, but he still gets too excited if other people give him attention. Just be aware when taking him to places with other people he might get a little jumpy if people start giving him attention.
- He is normally OK with other dogs, and please give him opportunities to socialize when you have a chance. Just be careful if he is off the leash and sees a dog chasing a ball, he will definitely go for it.

**Cuddles**

- Cotton loves cuddles, staying on the lap for long periods of time, or take a nap close to his human, either on his bed or on the sofa. I hope you enjoy his company, he is a lovely dog.

**Bad Behaviour**

- Cotton is a very well-behaved dog. I am quite strict with him and he already got the gist of it, so he rarely presents bad behaviour. Some things that might happen:
  - Chewing: Never happened on furniture, shoes, etc. the only exception, one time the sitter had a plant, and he tried to nibbled it. If it happens, please tell him off immediately, he normally is very obedient and will not go back to try to chew the same place again. **UPDATE: He recently started chewing sandals. Please keep yours away from him. It might be just a phase, but for now, we are going through this.**
  - Barking: I tell him off in every opportunity if he tries to bark, unless it is playtime. We don't want a barker at home as we live in a flat and I am very conscious of neighbours. Hopefully it will not happen, but if it does and it is not playtime, please feel free to be firm and tell him off.
  - Little bites: This is the way he likes to play. I let him bite at some extent during play time, but when I say "**Cotton,** **no biting**" or "**Cotton, calm down**" and shush him, he understands and stops immediately. Feel free to tell him off if he is crossing the line.

**Tear Stains**

- If you could please clean his eye with the Tear Stain remover once a day that I will take to avoid accumulating the stains on his face, I would be very grateful. The secret for this is consistency and as long you do it once a day that should be more than enough. Make sure to dry his face after cleaning to avoid moisture accumulating on his nose that could cause bacteria to settle.
